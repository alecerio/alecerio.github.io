About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Technical University of Denmark, where I work on embedded AI with a focus on quantization and adaptive neural networks capable of dynamically adjusting their behavior on-device. My research targets efficient deployment of neural networks on resource-constrained hardware, addressing challenges related to performance, memory footprint, and energy efficiency.

Previously, I was an Industrial PhD student at Jabra and the Technical University of Denmark, working on TinyML and neural network deployment. My work included quantization techniques, automated C code generation, and Neural Architecture Search for optimizing models under strict hardware constraints.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a DSP software developer at GN Hearing, where I developed and verified audio algorithms for embedded systems. With a background in C/C++ development and digital signal processing, I apply this expertise to embedded AI, particularly for audio applications such as speech enhancement and denoising, as well as emerging use cases like microplastic detection in water.

Through my involvement in Convolve and REINS projects, I gained experience working across industry and academia, strengthening my ability to contribute in multidisciplinary and international environments. I am passionate about bridging low-level engineering and modern AI to enable advanced neural networks to run efficiently on real-world devices.
